About me

I'm Mustafa Ascha, a PhD student in Clinical and Translational Science at the Center for Clinical Investigation and Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics at Case Western Reserve University.

To sum up my interests, I like to make things work. If I had a dremel when I was a kid, I would probably sand down a square peg to fit a round hole. Nowadays, I like to make information fit models: healthcare data (sequences analyses blog post to come), arbitrary digital data , analog signals (guitar effects post to come).

To see a more comprehensive list of my work, check out my CV or my github.
