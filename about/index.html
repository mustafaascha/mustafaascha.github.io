About me

I'm Mustafa Ascha, a PhD student in Clinical and Translational Science at the Center for Clinical Investigation and Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics at Case Western Reserve University. I have a wide range of interests, but my research interests involve the use of electronic health records and billing claims to produce actionable intelligence for medical professionals.

I have a Bachelor's degree in Economics with a minor in Philosophy and a Master's in Medical Physiology, both from Case Western Reserve University. Since I was an undergraduate, I've been interested in the process of making sense of evidence. When I was an undergraduate, I wanted to learn about modeling--but that is a general pursuit, so I was forced to choose to study how we model the distribution of resources. As a Master's student, I studied how we model human biological function. Now, I'm studying how to best model the application of resource distribution to human biology.

I've done some work related to my areas of interest, but I'll only share two projects here. My first publication was in 2009, a paper that reviewed rates of liver cancer in patients who do not have a history of alcoholism or viral infection. This publication has over five hundred and fifty citations, which is probably because the cancer-causing condition is on the rise. I'm really lucky my mentor is smart and had great intuition for what's important. I did the data collection and preparation, and wrote/edited the article itself.

The second article that I'm happy was published is a review of immunosuppression after liver transplantation.. I had to learn a lot to write this article, it was pretty awesome. This is from 2016, with an author who I met during that first study from 2009. Again, this article addresses growing public health issues, specifically the impact of immunosuppressants on diabetes and kidney disease.

There are a couple more articles going out that should better reflect my research interests. I only started really learning R last year, but since then have become enamored by R and computer science, generally. I was always a tinkerer, installing different linux distributions just to see what happens, but R has really been a perfect storm for my interest in modeling and tinkering.

To see a more comprehensive list of my work, check out my CV.

If you are interested in recommender systems or network analyses, [feel free to contact me](mustafa.ascha@gmail.com). I would be happy to discuss how to fit health data into new molds.
